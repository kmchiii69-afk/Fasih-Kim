# ICP Lead Scoring Criteria

This file is the rubric the bot uses to score every sales call lead.
Edit it freely — the bot reads it fresh on each call. No code changes needed.

The lead is scored on 5 factors. Each factor has a maximum point value (its
weight). The factor scores are added together for a total out of 100.

---

## Factors and weights

### 1. Buying power / budget — max 30 points
Can this lead realistically invest between $15,000 and $45,000+?
- Clear ability and willingness to invest $30k+ → 25–30
- Can do $15k–$30k, or willing but stretching → 15–24
- Budget is shaky, hesitant, or below $15k → 5–14
- Clearly cannot afford / not willing at this level → 0–4

### 2. Income level — max 25 points
Monthly income / business revenue. Qualified ICP is making $30k/mo or more.
- $30k/mo+ clearly established → 20–25
- $15k–$30k/mo → 12–19
- $5k–$15k/mo → 5–11
- Under $5k/mo or pre-revenue → 0–4

### 3. Pain point size — max 20 points
How big and pressing is the problem driving them to invest and commit?
- Severe, urgent pain they clearly want solved now → 16–20
- Real pain but not urgent → 10–15
- Mild or vague dissatisfaction → 4–9
- No real pain expressed → 0–3

### 4. Opportunity cost of staying stagnant — max 15 points
How much money are they losing by NOT acting? More lost = higher score.
- Large, quantified losses from inaction (clear $ left on table) → 12–15
- Meaningful but unquantified losses → 7–11
- Minor cost to waiting → 3–6
- Little or no cost to staying as they are → 0–2

### 5. Social proof / mutuals got results — max 10 points
Word of mouth — do friends/peers/mutuals vouch or have results?
- Strong: came via a happy mutual / peers got real results → 8–10
- Some: aware of others' results, soft referral → 4–7
- Minimal awareness → 1–3
- None → 0

---

## Scoring rules

- Score EACH factor on its own using the bands above.
- The total is the sum of the five factor scores (out of 100).
- **Missing data:** if a factor genuinely wasn't discussed on the call, mark that
  factor's score as null (unknown) — do NOT guess a number and do NOT count it as
  zero. The total is then the sum of the factors that WERE discussed, and should
  be read as provisional. Flag which factors were missing.
- Only use what's actually in the transcript. Never infer income or budget from
  vibes — if the closer didn't surface it, it's unknown.

---

## Score band meaning (for the total, when most factors are known)

- 85–100 — textbook fit. Budget, income, acute pain, real cost to waiting, social proof.
- 65–84 — strong fit. Meets budget and income, solid pain, missing one signal.
- 45–64 — partial fit. Money may be there but pain mild, or pain high but budget shaky.
- 25–44 — weak fit. Misses budget or income, limited urgency.
- 1–24 — not ICP. Can't afford and/or no real pain.
